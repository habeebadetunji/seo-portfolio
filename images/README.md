## Tips
- Use **TinyPNG** (tinypng.com) or **Squoosh** (squoosh.app) to compress before uploading — keeps load times fast.
- JPG works for photos; PNG is sharper for screenshots with lots of text. WebP is best of both — use it if your image editor supports export to WebP.
- For case study screenshots: crop to the headline metric (the big number). Blur any client-identifying info if needed.
- For `og-cover.jpg`: you can make a simple graphic in Canva with your name, title, and a key stat on a dark background.
